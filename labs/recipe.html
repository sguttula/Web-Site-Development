Apple Pie

An apple pie is a fruit pie in which the principal filling ingredient is apple. It is, on occasion,
served with whipped cream or ice cream on top, or alongside cheddar cheese. The pastry is generally used top-and-bottom,
making it a double-crust pie, the upper crust of which may be a circular shaped crust or a pastry lattice woven of strips;
exceptions are deep-dish apple pie with a top crust only. These are the general forms of making an apple pie,
but follow this recipe and you will love the taste!

Ingredients Directions
Note: Please be careful while using the oven or use adult guidence for safety. Feel free to make more than one and cherish this moment! Good luck with making a delicious apple pie!
  • 8 Granny Smith apples (peeled, cored & sliced)
  • ½ c. unsalted butter
  • 3 tbsp. All purposed flour
  • ¼ c. water
  • ½ c. white sugar
  • ½ c. brown sugar
  • 1 recipe pastry for a 9 in. double crust pie
  1. Preheat oven to 425° F (220° C)
  2. Melt butter in sausepan
  3. Stir flour to form a paste
  4. Add water, white sugar & brown sugar-wait till boil
  5. Reduce the temperature and let it sim
  6. Place the bottom crust in the pan
  7. Fill with apples & cover with lattice work crust
  8. Gently pour sugar and butter liquid over crust
  9. Pour slowly so liquid does not run off
  10. Bake for 15 min. in preheated oven
  11. Reduce temperature to 325° F (175° C)
  12. Continue baking for 35 to 45 min. until apples are soft
